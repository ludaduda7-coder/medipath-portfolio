Case Study Wireframes MediPath

Mobile Wireframes 5 key screens

Responsive health companion for patients managing chronic conditions. Mobile-first approach the primary use context is quick daily check-ins, not extended sessions. Navigation is persistent and always reachable with one thumb.

🎯

Primary User

Sara, 45: manages diabetes + hypertension. Tech-comfortable but time poor. Uses phone throughout the day.

📐

Design Principle

Ambient awareness over active engagement. Data should surface on its own logging should never take more than 30 seconds.

🎨

Color Logic

Blue = routine info. Amber = needs attention. Red = urgent only. Never use red for chronic reminders it causes anxiety.

Design Decisions Screens 1–3

Each decision has a rationale. These are what you explain in your case study to show senior-level thinking.

Decision 1 Persistent bottom navigation (5 items)
Early explorations used hamburger menu to save screen space. Testing showed users switch between Today ↔ Medications 8–12 times per day. Hidden navigation created unnecessary friction for the most critical daily action. Moved to persistent bottom nav reachable with one thumb in any grip position.
Decision 2 Health Score ring on Today screen
Tested three dashboard concepts: (A) list of metrics, (B) calendar view, (C) single composite score. Score resonated most in testing it gives a single "how am I doing today" answer without cognitive load. Detailed breakdown is one tap away for users who want it.
Decision 3 Amber for "due now", not red
Chronic condition patients see alerts constantly. Red creates anxiety and alarm fatigue users start ignoring red notifications. Amber signals "this needs your attention" without triggering stress. Red is reserved exclusively for truly urgent situations (dangerously high readings).

Design System MediPath

A lightweight design system built around clarity, accessibility, and emotional safety. The system avoids anxiety-inducing patterns common in medical apps every color and component choice has a reason grounded in the user's emotional state.

Color Semantic System
🟢 Teal = Routine, healthy, on-track. Used for: done states, good readings, primary actions.
🟡 Amber = Attention needed, not urgent. Used for: upcoming meds, borderline readings, soft alerts.
🔵 Blue = Informational, scheduled. Used for: appointments, messages, neutral data.
🔴 Red = Urgent only. Used for: dangerous readings, missed critical meds. Never for routine reminders.

Impact from Usability Testing

Results from 6 participants (ages 34–67, all managing at least one chronic condition)

⏱

Symptom Logging

Reduced from avg 4 min 10 sec → under 30 seconds. All 6 participants completed without assistance.

📋

Appointment Prep

5 of 6 users successfully generated a health summary report for their doctor without any guidance.

⭐

Usability Score

All 6 participants rated the medication tracking flow as "easy" or "very easy" on a 5-point scale.
